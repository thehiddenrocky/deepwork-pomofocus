# Blog 3: Draining the Shallows

Have you ever finished an 8-hour day feeling exhausted but realizing the "needle" hasn't moved on your most important project? That is the "Shallow Work" trap. 

Cal Newport defines "Shallow Work" as non-cognitively demanding, logistical-style tasks that are often performed while distracted. These tasks provide the *illusion* of productivity while delivering zero value.

To fight this, I added a "Protocol of Intent" to my focus tool: **The mandatory Session Note.**

### The 5-Second Reflection Ritual
Every time a focus session ends in PomoFocus, the cursor immediately jumps to a "Session Note" field. You can't just click "Start" again without acknowledging what you just did.

This forces a micro-audit of my time every 25 minutes. If I have to type "Cleared inbox" or "Checked Slack" into my log, it's a visual reminder that I'm wading in the shallows. 

### Logging Intentionality
By forcing this input, I’ve created a historical record of my intentionality. Looking at my logs, I can see the difference between a day filled with `"Refactoring core engine"` (Deep Work) and a day filled with `"Admin"`. 

The "Session Note" isn't for a manager; it's for me. It’s a guardrail that prevents me from sliding into "auto-pilot" mode. It turns every 25-minute block into a conscious decision to either go deep or stay shallow.

---
**Technical Takeaway:** Intentionality requires friction. By adding a small "check-in" step to your workflow, you can prevent hours of wasted time.
